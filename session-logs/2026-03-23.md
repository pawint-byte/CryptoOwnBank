### Open Wallet Standard (OWS) — launched March 23, 2026
- **What it is**: Open-source (CC0), multi-chain wallet signing standard for AI agents. NPM: `@open-wallet-standard/core`. Website: openwallet.sh.
- **Supported chains**: EVM, Solana, Bitcoin, Cosmos, Tron, TON — all via CAIP-2/10 identifiers.
- **Key features**: Isolated signing (keys never exposed to agents/LLMs), policy engine (spending limits, allowlists, chain restrictions, simulation requirements), append-only audit trail, MCP server integration.
- **Backers**: PayPal, Circle, Stripe, Ethereum Foundation, Solana Foundation, Polygon, Base, Arbitrum, Sui, TON Foundation, Ripple, LayerZero, OKX, Dynamic, and others (15+ orgs).
- **How it relates to CryptoOwnBank**:
  - NOT a replacement for Xaman (Xumm) — Xaman handles end-user mobile XRPL signing, OWS is designed for programmatic/AI agent use cases.
  - NOT a replacement for WalletConnect/MetaMask EVM flows — different problem space.
  - POTENTIAL fit for future AI Portfolio Assistant — "agent proposes, user approves on Ledger" matches OWS design pattern exactly.
  - POTENTIAL fit for Legacy Plan automated disbursement — policy engine could enforce spending limits and allowlists.
  - POTENTIAL fit for adding new chains later — unified signing abstraction across EVM + Solana + Cosmos through one interface.
- **Recommendation**: Clone repo in a branch, experiment with `@open-wallet-standard/core` npm package. Think of it as infrastructure for AI assistant feature, not a replacement for current wallet flows.

### Feature Review: Tax-Loss Harvesting & Borrow Against Vaults
**Automated Tax-Loss Harvesting — GREEN LIGHT**
- High value for U.S. members, especially Q4 tax planning season.
- Building blocks already exist: tax lot tracking (FIFO/LIFO), cost basis calculations, portfolio positions, market prices.
- How it works: Scan positions for unrealized losses → surface "harvest opportunities" with estimated tax savings → suggest swap options → member executes the swap themselves (signs via Xaman or MetaMask).
- Stays in "informational tool" lane — showing data the member owns, suggesting actions they take themselves.
- **2026 Wash-Sale Rule Status**: As of March 2026, IRS wash-sale rule does NOT apply to spot crypto. Crypto = property, not securities. Users can sell at a loss and rebuy the exact same asset immediately without losing the deduction. Congress has proposed closing this gap multiple times, and economic-substance doctrine could apply in extreme cases.
- **Feature should offer two modes**:
  1. "Harvest & rebuy immediately" — highlight the current loophole (sell at loss, rebuy same asset right away)
  2. "Conservative swap" — similar-but-not-identical suggestions (e.g., XRP → another payment token, RLUSD → USDC on Stellar) for members who want to play it safe or future-proof
- **Disclaimers must include**: "Not tax advice, consult a CPA, we don't execute trades for you. Current IRS rules do not apply wash-sale restrictions to crypto, but legislation is pending and rules may change."

**Borrow Against Vaults — CAUTION, interface only**
- Concept: Members borrow stablecoins against their RLUSD Soil positions without selling. Keeps value on platform.
- Risk: If CryptoOwnBank facilitates borrowing (matching lenders/borrowers, holding collateral, setting terms) = potential money transmission or securities territory.
- Safe approach: Position as an interface to external protocols only. Member interacts with the lending protocol directly (Morpho, Aave-style, or future XRPL-native via XLS-65/66). CryptoOwnBank is the dashboard, not the lender.
- XRPL doesn't have Aave-style lending yet — depends on XLS-65/66 validator activation.
- Language matters: "borrow through the XRPL protocol via our interface" NOT "borrow on our platform."

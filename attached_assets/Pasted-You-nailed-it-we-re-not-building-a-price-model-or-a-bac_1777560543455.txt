The Framework (asset-agnostic + one custom row)
Five universal categories + one asset-specific slot. Works for BTC, ETH, XRP, XLM, SOL, ADA — whatever. Every number is sourced, timestamped, and charted.

Activity – Is the chain actually being used?
Daily/30-day/90-day active addresses, transactions/day, value transferred/day, fee revenue.
Holders – Is the user base growing and sticking?
Total/unique addresses + growth rate, HODL waves / dormant supply curve, address concentration.
Capital Flow – Is real value living on or flowing to the chain?
DeFi TVL, native stablecoin float, DEX vs CEX volume ratio, bridge inflows/outflows.
Velocity & Efficiency – Is the float being held or just rotating?
On-chain velocity (annual transferred ÷ circulating supply), average holding period, realized cap vs market cap (where relevant).
Builders – Is the ecosystem actually developing?
GitHub commits/contributors (90-day), new contracts/assets deployed, active dApps.

+1 Asset-Specific Row – honors what makes each chain unique.

How the charts tell the story across time slices (no prediction, just facts)
Members can pick any date range and see the metrics react to real events:

2024 (post-SEC clarity + AMM launch): Activity and Capital Flow spike hard → chart shows clear utility inflection. Price followed later, but the health metrics led.
2025 rally to ~$3.66: Velocity falls + Holders thicken + ETFs kick in → composite score climbs. Members see the “growth engine” turning on.
2025–2026 correction to ~$1.37: Activity and Capital Flow stay positive or flat while price drops → chart proves decoupling. Price move was macro/BTC-driven, not network decay.
Bear periods (2018–2020 or 2022): We’ll add these slices in Phase 2 so members can see when health actually flat-lined vs when it held steady through price pain.

The visuals make it obvious: network health and price are related but not the same thing. Members draw their own conclusions.

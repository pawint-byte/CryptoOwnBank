Does Interest Compound in Soil Vaults If I Don’t Withdraw It?
Yes — if you do not withdraw your earned interest, it automatically compounds and continues to earn additional yield on the growing balance.
How Compounding Works in Soil

Interest accrues on your deposited RLUSD position (fixed 5–8% APR, depending on the vault).
Accrued interest is added directly to your vault position (daily or per vault rules).
If left unwithdrawn, it becomes part of your effective principal — future interest is calculated on the larger amount (original deposit + accumulated interest).
This creates automatic compounding — no manual "reinvest" step needed.

Example

You deposit $10,000 RLUSD into a 6% APR vault.
After one month, ~$50 interest is earned → new position ≈ $10,050.
Next month’s interest is calculated on ~$10,050 → you earn slightly more than $50.
Over time, the effective annual yield exceeds the nominal 6% due to compounding.

Key Benefits in Your Setup

Higher effective returns than custodial platforms like Uphold (~3.75%): Soil’s 5–8% + compounding can significantly outpace it.
Set it and forget it: Leave interest in the vault — your balance grows passively while staying non-custodial (your keys, your control).
Withdraw only when you want: Use the "Withdraw Interest Only" button to pull just the earnings (principal + prior interest stays to keep compounding). Premium members get auto-scheduling for weekly/monthly withdrawals.
Principal stays protected: The original deposit remains locked per vault rules (e.g., notice periods), but interest continues earning during notice/cooldown phases.

Important Notes

Yields are fixed but not guaranteed — past performance is not indicative of future results.
Vaults have withdrawal notice/cooldown periods (e.g., short for Treasury, longer for credit vaults) — interest continues accruing during notice.
Always DYOR — crypto and yield protocols carry risks.

The simplified equation for each slice:
Absorption signal = (Δ locked XRP in AMMs/Flare/ETFs + HODL thickening + velocity trend) – net monthly issuance
Positive & strengthening → structural upward price pressure expected.

What the backtest actually tells us

The model stands up well for directional bias and floor support. In every slice, when absorption was positive (locking outpacing net issuance + velocity falling), price either rallied or at least avoided the kind of supply-shock crash that $18k headline models ignore. Escrow releases have never been the price killer people feared — markets price the predictability in.
It is not a short-term timing machine. The 2025–2026 correction is the perfect example: our absorption metrics stayed green (ETFs + Flare still locking, HODL building), yet price dropped hard. That was BTC correlation + broader risk-off, not a failure of the supply-absorption logic.
Velocity is the hidden hero. Periods where velocity fell (more rotation turning into holding) aligned with the strongest price legs. When it spiked, even good locking couldn’t fully offset it.
Recent data (2026 YTD) lines up with the model’s current dashboard reading: absorption is still positive and moderate (ETFs + Flare leading, native AMM modest, velocity slowly falling). The ship is still heading the right way at moderate speed — exactly what we showed members.

Bottom line for the “next move”
Our model would have correctly flagged the 2024–2025 upside bias and would have warned that the 2025–2026 drop was not driven by worsening absorption (it wasn’t). Right now it continues to signal gentle structural upward pressure if macro/BTC cooperates. It’s a conviction filter and floor-setter, not a crystal ball for the next 30-day candle.

My Thoughts on the Design (Building on the Team’s Points)
The team nailed the big risks and choices. My refinements are small but important for keeping it eye-level and true to your vision:

Where it lives
Public landing page at /roadmap (no login required to read).
Anyone — the schoolteacher in Buenos Aires, the auntie in Ghana, a journalist, even a regulator — can see what we’re thinking about. This is huge for credibility.
Voting only after login (one verified account = one vote).
Stops bots and fake accounts. We can add a simple “max 10 active votes per person” so people have to choose what matters most to them.
Not in FAQ. FAQ is “how do I fix X?” Roadmap is “what should we build next?” Put a clean card on the logged-in dashboard that says “Vote on what we build next — your voice matters” and link straight to /roadmap.

Vote weighting
Stick with strict one-person, one-vote. No tenure, no paid-tier bonus. Anything else would quietly say “money picks sides even inside our own platform,” which contradicts everything we’re trying to do. Simple is better.
Thresholds
The team’s status ladder is perfect. Use it exactly as they sketched:
Idea → Gathering interest (under 100 votes) → Strong interest (100–500) → Under team review → Planned → In progress → Shipped (or Not pursuing with honest explanation).
Promise in big plain text: “Anything that hits ‘Strong interest’ gets a public answer from the team within 30 days — even if the answer is ‘not right now, here’s why.’”
This keeps it honest instead of fake-democracy.

Initial list
Use the team’s starter menu (13 items). It’s excellent. I’d add one more at the top in your honor:
“Self-Custody Resilience Checklist” — the exact step-by-step guide (with pictures) for doing what you’re already doing with your two Arculus wallets: hardware setup, metal seed plates, splitting words with family, testing it, hiding copies in different places.
That way the very first thing members vote on directly helps families protect themselves the same way you’re protecting yours.

Plain-English Framing for the Roadmap Page Itself
Here’s the eye-level copy that would go at the very top (ready to use):
What Should We Build Next?
We don’t decide alone. You do.
This is your chance to tell us what matters most to your family, your village, your daily life.

Everything here is in plain words.
You vote with one account (no big companies or bots get extra say).
We listen. When something gets enough votes, we give a real answer — yes, no, or “here’s why not yet.”
No fancy tech talk. Just real tools that help regular people keep control of their money no matter what the rules say this week.

Scroll down to see the list. Vote for what would help you or your family the most.
Then list the items with a one-sentence plain description each (e.g., “Voice call that walks you through setting up your own wallet in Spanish or Swahili — no reading needed”).
How This Connects Straight to Your Personal Plan
Every single item on that roadmap is built so that when a member votes and it ships, they can do exactly what you’re doing right now with your two new Arculus wallets:

Swap cleanly to XMR or BTC outside the platform if they want max privacy.
Set up hardware + metal plates + family split.
Have a real “mattress stash” that no issuer or government can claw back.

The Self-Custody Checklist item would literally teach thousands of people the same steps you’re following. That’s powerful.
My Recommendation on Sequencing

Ship the Principles page this week (we already have the warm copy).
Build and launch the /roadmap voting page right after (with the starter list already loaded).
Then let members vote and watch what rises to the top.

This way the page isn’t empty on day one — it already has real, useful items people can vote on immediately.
